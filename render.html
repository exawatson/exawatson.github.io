DPG Live in Tokyo and Tamaonsen Live in Kagoshima!

I can’t possibly explain everything that happened last week, but here’s a photo summary of one of the craziest weeks of my life…

I initially went to Shibuya again to see DenpaGirl perform live, and AO made a guest performance! The show was wonderful, but I didn’t get a chance to see AO afterward. It was still a great time with my friends though and despite getting in fairly late we were pretty close to the front!

During my time at the DPG live, my friends and I discovered AO and his Tamaonsen (Tohou Hip-Hop Remix Fan-Music Crew) was doing a sale and live performance that Sunday! But I was set to leave back for Hong Kong on Wednesday. Plus, the live show was in Kyushu- the southern island of Japan. I told myself I’d have to skip out on this one…

…Until I missed my flight check in by 2 minutes and they refused to let me on the plane. Budget airlines, man. Thanks to the help of my incredible friends (because ya boy was flat ass broke) I could afford a ticket out of the country on Sunday just before I got back into classes. But because of an incredibly generous gift from one of my dear friends, I was actually able to afford a Shinkansen ticket to Kyushu that Saturday, and a place to stay at a friends house until then. I wondered to myself… Was… Was this supposed to happen…??

Someone, or something out there knew that Kagoshima was where I wanted to go. Where I needed to go… That only seeing DPG had left me feeling unsatisfied. And it was on the 7 hour train there that I discovered none of my Japanese friends were actually going to the live show. Not only that, but I was alone. Me, who speaks very little Japanese, alone on the island of Kyushu, on the southern tip of Japan. I was incredibly nervous.

But I eventually found my way there. The convention was… Smaller than any con I’ve ever been to, even in America. Nothing was in English, but I knew enough Japanese to purchase entry. And then I saw AO and his crew, behind one of the tables, selling their music. I don’t want to ever forget what that felt like. To have traveled so far, to have endured so much (only thanks to the help of those who care about me, of course) and to have gone all that way to a place I was utterly physically alone in, to see AO and support him again. As usual, he greeted me with a wave and a smile. We did our best to communicate with each other, difficult as it was. As usual, him and his crew treated me so nicely, despite the fact I was a shaking nervous wreck lmao but I eventually got comfortable with everyone. And I got to enjoy their live show! I was even allowed to watch them prepare for the performance. It was unreal. And I was right at the very front for that one, too. I’d never seen AO perform so naturally and smile so easily any of the times id seen him perform live before. It may have been because the crowd didn’t break 50 people, and the con was incredibly small. I didn’t get the feeling he was under pressure… He was truly having fun. And so, I had fun too.

No matter what, I don’t regret Kyushu. I made many mistakes on my way there. But I learned so much about myself, about my friends, about what it means to say fuck it sometimes and do what feels right. But also about how scary things can get and the people it affects when you let yourself become irresponsible…

I’m not perfect, and I’m certainly nothing without my friends and the people around me. Though I went alone, and that did take courage, it was only because people had my back. That was something I never expected for some reason.

Whatever the fuck last week in Japan was……. I’m glad it happened.

-Karen
